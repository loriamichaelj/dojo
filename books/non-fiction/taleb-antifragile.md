# Antifragile

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Nassim Nicholas Taleb |
| **Published** | 2012 |

## Synopsis

*Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder* is the capstone of Taleb's *Incerto*
series. It introduces a third category beyond fragile and robust: the *antifragile* —
systems that don't merely withstand volatility, randomness, and stress but actually
improve because of them. Taleb argues that we systematically build fragile systems
by suppressing small stressors (bailouts, over-intervention, debt, centralization),
which transfers risk into rare, catastrophic blow-ups. He champions optionality,
the "barbell" strategy, *via negativa* (improvement by removal), and skin in the
game as ways to thrive under uncertainty. Spanning markets, medicine, politics, and
personal life, it is a worldview for acting wisely in a world we cannot predict.
