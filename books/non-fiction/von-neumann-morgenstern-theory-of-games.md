# Theory of Games and Economic Behavior

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | John von Neumann and Oskar Morgenstern |
| **Published** | 1944 |

## Synopsis

The foundational text of game theory. Von Neumann and Morgenstern argue that
economic behavior is best modeled as strategic interaction between rational agents,
and build the mathematical apparatus to do so. The book formalizes games of
strategy, proves the minimax theorem for two-person zero-sum games, and introduces
the von Neumann–Morgenstern expected-utility theorem, which axiomatizes rational
choice under uncertainty. It also develops cooperative game theory through the
notions of coalitions, characteristic functions, and stable sets. Dense and
rigorous, it reframed economics around equilibrium and strategic reasoning, and its
ideas underpin modern microeconomics, auction theory, and quantitative finance.
