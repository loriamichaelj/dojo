# The Master and His Emissary

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Iain McGilchrist |
| **Published** | 2009 |

## Synopsis

*The Master and His Emissary: The Divided Brain and the Making of the Western World*
argues that the two hemispheres of the brain embody two fundamentally different modes
of attending to reality — not the tired "logic vs. creativity" cliché, but a deeper
divide. The right hemisphere attends to the whole, the living, the contextual, and
the implicit; the left abstracts, categorizes, manipulates, and renders the world as
explicit, fixed, and usable. McGilchrist's title parable casts the right hemisphere as
the wise "Master" and the left as the "Emissary" that, having forgotten its servant
role, usurps the whole. The book's second half reads Western intellectual history —
from ancient Greece through the Reformation, Enlightenment, and modernity — as a
story of the left hemisphere's growing, and dangerous, dominance. It is the rigorous
neuroscientific companion to the myth-and-meaning concerns of *Maps of Meaning*.
