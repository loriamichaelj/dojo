# Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Douglas R. Hofstadter |
| **Published** | 1979 |

## Synopsis

*Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid* asks how meaning, consciousness, and
the sense of an "I" can arise from inanimate matter following formal rules. Hofstadter
weaves together the logician Kurt Gödel, the artist M. C. Escher, and the composer
Johann Sebastian Bach to expose a shared pattern — the *strange loop*, a hierarchy
that twists back to reference itself. Its spine is Gödel's incompleteness theorems,
which show that any sufficiently powerful formal system contains true statements it
cannot prove, and Hofstadter argues the same self-referential trick underlies how
symbols, recursion, and feedback give rise to minds. Ranging across formal systems,
recursion, molecular biology, Zen, and artificial intelligence — and alternating
exposition with playful Achilles-and-Tortoise dialogues — it bridges the gap between
information and meaning.
