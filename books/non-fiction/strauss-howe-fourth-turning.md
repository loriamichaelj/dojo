# The Fourth Turning

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | William Strauss and Neil Howe |
| **Published** | 1997 |

## Synopsis

*The Fourth Turning: An American Prophecy* argues that history moves not in a
straight line but in recurring cycles roughly the length of a human life (~80–100
years), called *saecula*. Each saeculum unfolds through four "turnings" — a High, an
Awakening, an Unraveling, and a Crisis — driven by the predictable aging of four
generational archetypes (Prophet, Nomad, Hero, Artist). Strauss and Howe contend
that modern societies periodically reach a "Fourth Turning," a decisive era of
upheaval in which old institutions are torn down and a new civic order is forged.
Written in 1997, the book forecasts a major crisis in the early twenty-first century
and frames generational dynamics as a lens for anticipating social and economic
regime change.
