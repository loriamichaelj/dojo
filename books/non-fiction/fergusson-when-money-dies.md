# When Money Dies

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Adam Fergusson |
| **Published** | 1975 |

## Synopsis

*When Money Dies: The Nightmare of Deficit Spending, Devaluation, and Hyperinflation
in Weimar Germany* is a narrative history of the collapse of the German mark between
1918 and 1923. Drawing on diaries, letters, and contemporary accounts, Fergusson
chronicles how a government financing its deficits by printing money destroyed the
savings, social order, and trust of an entire nation — wages paid twice a day,
wheelbarrows of banknotes, the wiping out of the middle class, and the political
radicalization that followed. It is the empirical case study behind the sound-money
arguments of *The Bitcoin Standard* and *Human Action*, and a vivid portrait of the
kind of monetary crisis that cyclical histories like *The Fourth Turning* warn of.
