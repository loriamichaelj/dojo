# The (Mis)Behavior of Markets

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Benoit B. Mandelbrot and Richard L. Hudson |
| **Published** | 2004 |

## Synopsis

*The (Mis)Behavior of Markets: A Fractal View of Financial Turbulence* is the father
of fractal geometry's assault on orthodox quantitative finance. Mandelbrot argues
that the bell curve and the random-walk models underlying modern portfolio theory,
Black–Scholes, and Value-at-Risk drastically understate real market risk: price
changes are not mild and independent but wild, fat-tailed, and clustered, with
volatility that arrives in bursts and dependence across time. Drawing on his work on
fractals and power laws, he shows that markets exhibit self-similarity across scales
and far more extreme moves than Gaussian models admit. The book is both a critique of
the foundations most quants are taught and a call for risk models that take
turbulence seriously.
