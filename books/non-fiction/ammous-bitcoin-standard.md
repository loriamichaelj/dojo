# The Bitcoin Standard

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Saifedean Ammous |
| **Published** | 2018 |

## Synopsis

*The Bitcoin Standard: The Decentralized Alternative to Central Banking* frames
Bitcoin as the latest advance in a long history of monetary technology. Ammous
traces money from seashells and stones through gold, arguing that "hardness" —
resistance to supply inflation, captured by a high stock-to-flow ratio — is what
makes a good store of value, and that the shift to government fiat severed money
from that discipline. He presents Bitcoin's fixed 21-million supply and
decentralized settlement as a digital form of sound money, and explores the
consequences for saving, time preference, capital accumulation, and the role of
central banks. Part monetary history, part Austrian-economics argument, it is a
foundational text in the case for Bitcoin as hard money.
