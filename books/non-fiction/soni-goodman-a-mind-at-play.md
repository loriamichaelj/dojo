## Synopsis

*A Mind at Play: How Claude Shannon Invented the Information Age* is a biography of
the mathematician and engineer who founded information theory. Soni and Goodman
trace Shannon's path from rural Michigan to MIT and Bell Labs, where his 1948 paper
*A Mathematical Theory of Communication* introduced the bit as the fundamental unit
of information and established the limits of data compression and reliable
transmission — the theoretical bedrock of all digital communication. The book also
captures Shannon the playful polymath: a juggler, unicyclist, and tinkerer who built
chess machines and a mechanical mouse, and who treated curiosity as its own reward.
It is both a portrait of a singular mind and an account of how information became a
measurable quantity.
