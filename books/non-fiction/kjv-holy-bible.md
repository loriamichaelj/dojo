## Synopsis

The King James Version (KJV), also known as the Authorized Version, is an English
translation of the Christian Bible produced for the Church of England and first
published in 1611. Translated by 47 scholars working from Hebrew, Aramaic, and
Greek texts, it comprises the Old and New Testaments — a collection of historical
narrative, law, poetry, prophecy, gospel, and epistle. Its measured, rhythmic
prose has profoundly shaped English literature and language for over four
centuries.
