| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Jon Gertner |

## Synopsis

*The Idea Factory: Bell Labs and the Great Age of American Innovation* tells the
story of the research institution that produced much of the foundational technology
of the modern world. Gertner profiles the scientists and managers — Mervin Kelly,
Claude Shannon, William Shockley, John Bardeen, and others — whose work yielded the
transistor, information theory, the laser, the solar cell, communications
satellites, and Unix. More than a chronicle of inventions, the book examines what
made Bell Labs so productive: long time horizons, the mixing of theorists and
engineers, freedom to pursue fundamental problems, and a culture that turned basic
research into deployed technology. It is both a history of innovation and a study of
how breakthrough ideas are organized and funded.
