# The Sovereign Individual

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | James Dale Davidson and William Rees-Mogg |
| **Published** | 1997 |

## Synopsis

*The Sovereign Individual: Mastering the Transition to the Information Age* argues
that the shift from an industrial to an information economy will erode the
nation-state's monopoly on power much as gunpowder once dismantled feudalism. The
authors predict that as wealth and work move online and beyond the reach of
territorial taxation, the most capable individuals will gain unprecedented autonomy
— transacting in digital, government-independent money and choosing jurisdictions
like consumers. Written in 1997, the book anticipates cryptography-based currencies,
remote work, and the decline of mass politics, framing these as a fundamental
renegotiation of the relationship between citizens and states.
