# Human Action

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Ludwig von Mises |
| **Published** | 1949 |

## Synopsis

*Human Action: A Treatise on Economics* is the central work of the Austrian School
and Ludwig von Mises's systematic statement of praxeology — the study of purposeful
human behavior. Mises derives economics deductively from the axiom that individuals
act to remove felt unease, building up from subjective value and marginal utility to
prices, money, capital, interest, and the role of the entrepreneur. He argues that
rational economic calculation is impossible under socialism without market prices,
defends the price system and sound money, and explains the business cycle as a
consequence of credit expansion. A sweeping, methodologically distinctive defense of
free-market capitalism and a counterpoint to mainstream mathematical economics.
