|---|---|

## Synopsis

*Supernatural: Meetings with the Ancient Teachers of Mankind* investigates the
origins of human religion, mythology, and symbolic culture. Hancock argues that the
sudden flourishing of cave art and complex belief systems in the Upper Paleolithic
coincided with humanity's encounter with altered states of consciousness, often
induced by psychoactive plants such as those in the Amazonian brew ayahuasca. He
draws parallels between the imagery of prehistoric art, shamanic visions, and modern
reports of contact with otherworldly "beings," proposing that these recurring
experiences shaped the birth of spirituality. Speculative and controversial, the
book blends archaeology, anthropology, and first-person accounts of psychedelic
experience into a single sweeping thesis.
