## Synopsis

*Fortune's Formula: The Untold Story of the Scientific Betting System That Beat the
Casinos and Wall Street* traces the Kelly criterion from information theory to the
gambling table and the stock market. Poundstone tells how Bell Labs physicist John
Kelly, building on Claude Shannon's work, derived a formula for sizing bets to
maximize the long-run growth rate of capital, and how mathematician Edward Thorp
turned it into a system that beat blackjack and then ran a market-neutral hedge fund.
Along the way it weaves in Shannon, Thorp, organized crime, and the academic feud
between Kelly-style growth optimizing and orthodox portfolio theory. It is a history
of where information theory, probability, and quantitative finance meet — the bridge
between *A Mind at Play* and the math of investing.
