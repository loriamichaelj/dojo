# Snow Crash

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Neal Stephenson |
| **Published** | 1992 |

## Synopsis

A lean, fast cyberpunk novel set in a near-future America fractured into corporate
city-states and franchised sovereignties, where a hacker-swordsman named Hiro
Protagonist chases a metaphysical computer virus that crosses between the Metaverse
and the human mind. Beneath the action runs a serious argument about language,
information, and myth as code — Sumerian *me*, the Tower of Babel, and the brain as a
programmable machine — that connects to the information theory of *A Mind at Play*
and the myth-and-meaning thread of Peterson and McGilchrist. The decentralized,
state-eroding world it depicts also previews *The Sovereign Individual*. The leanest
entry point to Stephenson before committing to the 900-page *Cryptonomicon*.
