# Cryptonomicon

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Neal Stephenson |
| **Published** | 1999 |

## Synopsis

*Cryptonomicon* braids two timelines: WWII codebreakers — including a fictionalized
Lawrence Waterhouse who works alongside Alan Turing — racing to exploit and protect
the Allied cryptographic edge, and their descendants in the late 1990s building a
Southeast Asian "data haven" and launching a gold-backed digital currency to escape
state monetary control. It is the rare novel that sits at the exact intersection of
this shelf: the cryptography and entropy of *A Mind at Play* and *Gödel, Escher,
Bach*, the sound-money politics of *The Bitcoin Standard* and *The Sovereign
Individual*, and long, loving digressions on number theory and probability that
rhyme with Taleb and Poundstone. Written in 1999, it effectively predicted the
cypherpunk-to-Bitcoin arc a decade early.
