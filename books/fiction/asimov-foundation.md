# Foundation

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Isaac Asimov |
| **Published** | 1951 |

## Synopsis

The opening novel of Asimov's *Foundation* series, in which mathematician Hari Seldon
develops "psychohistory" — a statistical science that predicts the behavior of large
populations well enough to forecast the fall of a galactic empire and chart a path to
shorten the dark age that follows. The premise that civilizations move through
predictable, modelable cycles maps directly onto the generational theory of *The
Fourth Turning*, while psychohistory itself is a fable about probability, large
numbers, and the limits of prediction that resonates with Taleb's *Antifragile* and
Mandelbrot's *The Misbehavior of Markets*. A foundational text on cyclical history
told as fiction.
