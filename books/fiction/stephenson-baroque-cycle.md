# The Baroque Cycle

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | Neal Stephenson |
| **Published** | 2003–2004 |

## Synopsis

A three-volume historical epic (*Quicksilver*, *The Confusion*, *The System of the
World*) set in the late 17th and early 18th centuries, following natural
philosophers, vagabonds, and financiers through the Scientific Revolution and the
literal invention of modern money and finance. Isaac Newton, Gottfried Leibniz, and
a cast of alchemists and coiners populate a story about the birth of calculus, the
gold standard, and the credit systems that became modern capital markets. Shares its
DNA with *Cryptonomicon* — same families, same obsessions — but reaches back to the
historical roots of the sound-money and quantitative threads running through *The
Bitcoin Standard*, *Wealth of Nations*, and *The Misbehavior of Markets*. Longer and
more historical; a deeper dive for after *Cryptonomicon*.
